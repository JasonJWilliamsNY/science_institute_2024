Lecture

Electrophoresis and sequencing

In these classes we will cover two important experimental techniques in the molecular biology lab. Sanger DNA Sequencing is a common method for determining the sequence of a DNA strand. Before sequencing, but after PCR we often need to determine what DNA we have amplified in the lab (Is the fragment the correct size? Have we obtained enough to sequence? Is the product pure?). These questions can be answered by electrophoresis.

Questions to answer about electrophoresis

  1. What type of gel is used?
  2. What is the loading dye and what is the purpose?
  3. How does DNA migrate in an electric field?
  4. How do we view the stained DNA bands; what stain, what light source is used?
