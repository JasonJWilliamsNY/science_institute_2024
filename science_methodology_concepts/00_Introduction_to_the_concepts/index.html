Introduction to these concepts

This and the other 10 concepts are ideas that we will focus on in this science research course. There are a few reasons these concepts have been chosen and presented in this way. These concepts are (hopefully):

  • Foundational: appear again and again when thinking about and doing science.

  • Memorable: easy to recall so to help spark ideas and think about problems.

  • Incomplete: able to be combined, extended, and embellished, guiding you to identify new concepts.

Concepts should prompt you to ask questions about the purpose of your project, what prior knowledge reveals, your choice of methods, how conclusions are drawn, and those conclusions should be followed up on.

Each concept has some introduction, points to discuss in class, activities, and some questions (which may also be assigned pre/post class).

It is also useful to remember that concepts may apply slightly differently before you have begun a research project, during the project, and once the research/experiment portion of the project has been completed.
