Discussion

This is a gigantic topic we will never finish in class. It's exciting because there is so much to learn, and as we discuss in class our unique combination of experiences and questions will guide our conversation deeper down some directions, leaving others for future consideration.

Following from our first concept study on science, we have a few major outline points our conversation needs to touch on.

Questions and method

Science is not unique in starting with questions. Nearly every human endeavor begins with. But what does it mean for a question to be a scientific question?

  • How many types of questions can you name? How would you classify questions?
  • Are there any of these questions that you think are essential or excluded from science?

In order to do its work, science is also concerned with method. This in itself does not make science special (again - almost every approach to inquiry has its own method)

  • What do you think are the essential components of the scientific method?
  • Are all of these steps always used (must some of them always be used)?

Hypothesis, model, and theory

One of the unique components of the scientific method is hypothesis (ὑπόθεσις - “I place under, set before, suggest”).A hypothesis can start out by using induction to take research and general observations to pose a theory or law that can be tested. Incidentally, it is here where we can first look to dispose of the notion that science is derived from the facts. All of our observations will inductively lead us to a theory or hypothetical statement.

  • What differentiates a hypothesis from a guess?

On our way to rejecting a hypothesis (what about accepting it?) we may come up with some explanation that we think allow us to explain phenomena we are investigating.

  • What is a model?
  • How should a model be stated?

When we have (perhaps several) model(s) we are able to explain and predict several phenomena. This is often the basis for saying we have a theory.

  • How do we prove a theory?
  • Should a theory be believed?
  • What is the role of falsification?

Logic

As mentioned in our science concept notes, reasoning is the tool we use to think about science. But reasoning and logic comes in many different forms.

  • What is induction?
  • What is deduction?
  • Is one superior to the other?
