Quiz Review Sheet

Quiz: PTC and PCR

Ground rules/instructions

  1. No talking - During the quiz there is absolutely no talking. If you have a question, please raise your hand. If I can fairly answer your question (for example about a ambiguous statement or error) I will and will inform the other quiz takers.
  2. Spread out - you must have an empty chair to your right and left.
  3. You must bring your Chromebook - quiz will be online

Review

Review the following Here are the key items and study points you should review for the quiz:

  • PTC lab protocol
  • PTC slides

You should be able to define and/or explain:

  • What is the gene we examined in the PTC laboratory?
  • How did we get DNA from our cheek cells? (i.e. saline rinse, chelex, boiling)
  • Why PCR is a useful technique in molecular biology
  • What the stages of the PCR process are and what happens at each stage (e.g. Denature, anneal, extend)
  • What the chemical components of PCR are (e.g. polymerase, primer, template DNA, etc.) and what their purpose is.
  • What is a single-nucleotide polymorphisim (SNP)?
  • What is a transition, transversion, indel?
  • How DNA was isolated for mtDNA sequencing (e.g. saline rinse and chelex)
  • What the stages of the PCR process are and what happens at each stage (e.g. Denature, anneal, extend)?
  • What the chemical components of PCR are (e.g. polymerase, primer, template DNA, etc.) and what their purpose is?
  • You should be able to interpret a gel from a PTC lab
